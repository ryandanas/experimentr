Specification

We consider 3 classes: Persons, Departments, and Salaries.

The class of Persons is partitioned into 2 non-overlapping subclasses: Employees, and Bosses.

We make the following assumptions:

  1. For each Salary there is a set of Employees that it is paid_to.
  2. A Person can_raise a Salary only if either the Person is a manager_for the Department affiliated_with the Salary or the Person is the Boss who is the head_of that Department.
  3. For each Salary there is a single Department affiliated_with it.
  4. For each Department there is a single Boss who is the head_of it.
  5. For each Department there is a set of Employees who are the managers_for it.

Assertion

Here is the assertion that we thought would be true:

There cannot exist a Person who can_raise a Salary paid_to them.
